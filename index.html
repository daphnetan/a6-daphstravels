Having grown up abroad alongside adventurous parents, I have always loved exploring the world outside my own. Every year since my junior year of college has included some weeks abroad.

I never thought anyone was interested in where I’ve gone or seen, but many friends have asked me time and time again for city recommendations or for pictures from these travels. And so, I’ve decided to make this site as a digital keepsake, a scrapbook of memories and tidbits that I hope benefits friends and strangers.

All information has been pulled from hilarious Google docs created by my friends and I—I can’t take credit for all the planning, that’s for sure. None of this is exhaustive or representative of where you should explore, eat, or experience. It's merely a place to start since I know how hard it can be to start from scratch.

Check back for more travel guides in the future. There’s just way too many sunsets to be seen and experienced.

Where do you want to travel next?

I’d like to explore the United States more. I feel as though there’s so many places I haven’t even seen even though I live in this country. Post-graduation, I’d like to complete a cross-country road trip. Oh, and New Zealand. That is the dream right now, outside of the United States.

What do you use to plan your trips?

I use Google all the time and look for picturesque places on Instagram. Google Docs and Maps are also really great for collaborating with friends. Docs is for the list of places and the details; maps is for helping us identify where places are in relation to our location and each other so we can maximize our time. Sometimes I use Google Sheets if I’m feeling more OCD about things.

What’s your travel style?

I typically like a mix of culture and food. The amount of things to do in the city or town dictates the pace. I do tend to like to see almost everything and create an exhaustive list. Typically, when the actual trip rolls around, my friends and I will look at a list and map to figure out what’s realistic for the day.

What do you like to plan for during your trips?

I’ve really been into bike rides lately when traveling. It feels like I experience the city like a local. I also like to make sure I get one meal of what the locals eat. Local grocery stores are always a go-to as well because I enjoy seeing how a product translates in a completely different culture.

How do you figure out housing?

I’m typically not the person who figures out housing on my trip. My friends and I have used Airbnb and Booking.com. We always figure out which neighborhoods we’d like to live in first then look at the options within that neighborhood. Budget does dictate almost every decision with this specific aspect of travel though.

How do you pack for your trips?

Light. Typically two pairs of jeans, three tops, a dress, one pair of “nice” shoes, and my Nikes. Most recently, I got Cotopaxi Allpa 35L Travel Pack and it’s amazing. No regrets there.

How do you document your travels?

The past four years, I’ve just carried my 35mm film camera with a 40mm lens. The camera is really light and the lens is so flat. So far, it’s my favorite combo in terms of convenience and picture quality. I love street photography so this is what’s allowed me to take pictures best without the bulk of my wedding photography gear. Depending on the trip, I’ll allot 1 roll of film for every 2 days.
